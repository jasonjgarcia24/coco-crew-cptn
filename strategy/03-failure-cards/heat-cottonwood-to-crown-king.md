# 🔥 HEAT — Cottonwood Creek → Crown King (M7.4 → M36.6)

**Print + laminate. One copy in start bag, one in CK drop bag, one on person.**

## What this is
The 25 mi between Cottonwood Creek (M7.4) and Crown King (M36.6) is the hottest, hardest, most exposed section of the course. Mid-pack 10 hrs. Back-of-pack 13+ hrs. Two intermediate water stations, **1L cap each**.

This is the section that pulls more runners from Cocodona than any other.

## Pre-leave Cottonwood (M7.4) — non-negotiable
- [ ] **4 LITERS minimum** of water on person (verified)
- [ ] Sun gear deployed: hat, glasses, sleeves, sunscreen reapplied
- [ ] Salt / electrolyte capsules accessible
- [ ] Calories accessible (hand-flask gel / chews / bar)
- [ ] Required Gear on person

## On the trail — rules
1. **Drink BEFORE thirsty.** Thirst at this temp = already dehydrated.
2. **Salt every 30 min** regardless of how you feel.
3. **Walk every uphill.** Pace below threshold all day.
4. **Shade > everything.** Sit in shade if available, even if it costs 10 min.
5. **Pour water on hat/sleeves** to cool by evaporation — but don't waste drinking water if intake at risk.

## At water stations (M10.4, M24.6) — 1L cap
- Take 1L. Drink some immediately. Pour any excess on hat/sleeves.
- DON'T linger past what's necessary. Don't sit if you can stand.

## Red flags — STOP and assess
- **Stop sweating** in the heat → heat exhaustion / heat stroke risk → SHADE NOW, ICE NOW, lower core temp
- **Mental fog, slurred speech** → SHADE, ICE, fluids w/ salt
- **Cramping** that doesn't resolve in 5 min → salt + slow walk
- **No urination in 4+ hrs** → drink to color (pale yellow target)
- **Goosebumps in heat** → heat stroke imminent → STOP

## If wrecked at Lane Mtn (M32.5) before Crown King
- Use the medic
- Eat, drink, ice
- 4 mi to Crown King — walk it, don't push

## If you make it to Crown King
You survived the worst. Reward yourself with full recovery: medic + foot care + fueling + sleep decision.
