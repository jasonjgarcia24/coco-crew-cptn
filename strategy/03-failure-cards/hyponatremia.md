# 💧 HYPONATREMIA (low sodium)

**Print + laminate. Copy in start bag, CK bag, every heat-section bag.**

## What this is
Drinking too much water without enough sodium dilutes blood sodium → swelling, confusion, seizures, death. Most common in heat sections when runners over-drink without salt.

**This is preventable.** And it's the second-most-common reason runners are pulled from hot ultras (after heat exhaustion).

## Symptoms (early → late)
- **Early:** nausea, headache, swollen hands/fingers, swollen face, watch indents that don't fade, "puffy"
- **Mid:** confusion, slurred speech, lethargy unrelated to fatigue
- **Late:** seizure, unconsciousness — emergency

## Decision tree
- **Early symptoms only** → STOP drinking water → eat salt (electrolyte caps + salty food) → small sips ONLY → resume after 30 min once swelling resolves
- **Mid symptoms** → STOP at next AS → tell medic → no more plain water → salt + salty broth + slow recovery
- **Late symptoms** → emergency → contract Section I → DROP, medic, possibly 911

## Pre-empt — the rules
1. **Salt with EVERY calorie source.** No naked carbs.
2. **Drink to thirst + 1 cup/hour cap in heat** — don't chug
3. **Pee color: pale yellow target.** Clear = drinking too much. Dark = too little.
4. **Pre-loaded salt before big heat sections** (electrolyte cap before leaving Cottonwood)

## Sodium targets
Sweat rate baseline:
- **Day:** 1.0 L/hr × 757 mg/L = **757 mg/hr lost**
- **Night:** 0.5 L/hr × 757 mg/L = **378 mg/hr lost**

Intake target:
- **Day moderate:** 500 mg/hr (slight deficit; OK short term)
- **Day extreme heat (Cottonwood→CK, Verde Valley descent):** **700–900 mg/hr** (track to balance loss)
- **Night/cold:** 500 mg/hr (slight surplus; fine)

Sources to hit 700–900 mg/hr:
- 1× Personal mix (500 mg) + 1× Liquid IV (510 mg) per hour = 1,010 mg/hr
- 1× Personal mix (500 mg) + 1× salt cap (~250 mg) + 1× Neversecond (200 mg) = ~950 mg/hr

## Common Cocodona-specific traps
- Over-drinking at the M10.4 / M24.6 water stations because "it's free water"
- Forgetting salt because gels feel like enough
- Drinking plain water with bland gel-only fueling
- Cooler temps after Crown King → still need salt with calories

## DO NOT confuse with dehydration
**Symptoms can look similar early. Difference:**
- Dehydration: dark urine, very thirsty, dry mouth → drink + salt
- Hyponatremia: pale/clear urine, may have been drinking a lot, swelling, headache → salt + STOP drinking

When unsure → eat salt first. Sodium errs safer at this temperature than free water.
