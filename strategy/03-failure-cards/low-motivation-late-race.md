# 🧠 LOW MOTIVATION — LATE RACE

**Print + laminate. Copy in Sedona Posse drop bag, Munds Park drop bag, Fort Tuthill drop bag.**

## What this is
After ~150 mi, the body is mostly fine but the brain wants to quit. This is normal. Plan for it. Don't trust your "want to drop" signal at 3 AM after no sleep.

## Decision rule
**You are not allowed to drop on a feeling alone.** Per the contract: dropping requires a Section I (hard pull) symptom or a written reason on this card.

## When you want to drop — checklist before talking to AS staff
1. Eat something. 200+ cal.
2. Drink 500 ml + electrolyte
3. Sit for 5 min, eyes closed
4. Foot care if not done in last 2 AS
5. **Then** decide

If after 1–5 you still want to drop → write the reason on the back of this card and walk it to the AS captain.

## Reframes that work
- "I just need to get to the next AS." (Don't look further.)
- "Quitting is a long-term decision, not a 3-AM decision."
- "I will think about quitting at the NEXT AS — until then, walk."
- "The pain is real. The story about quitting is optional."
- "Future-me will not forgive present-me for a non-Section-I drop."

## Reframes that DON'T work (avoid)
- "Just push through" (no — eat, then push)
- "Toughen up" (no — assess physical state first)
- Comparing yourself to other runners (their race ≠ your race)

## Solo + crewless reminder
Nobody is here to talk you off the ledge. **The card is the talker.** Re-read it. The plan was made by well-rested-Jason. Trust the plan.

## DNF vs. DNFing — the lever
Per the rules: you can drop and re-enter the race if you return before sweepers and pick up your SPOT.

- Take a 1–2 hr sleep at AS
- Eat real food
- Reassess
- If you can move forward → re-enter and continue

This is NOT cheating. It's a tool. Use it before pulling for good.

## When to ACTUALLY drop (per contract Section I)
- Cardiac symptoms
- Vomiting blood
- Cannot keep fluids down 4+ hrs
- Visible serious injury
- Disorientation / mental status change beyond sleep deprivation

If none of those → eat → walk → next AS → reassess.
