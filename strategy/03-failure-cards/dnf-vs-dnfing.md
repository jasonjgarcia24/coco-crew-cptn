# 🔄 DNF vs. DNFing — THE LEVER

**Print + laminate. Copy in every drop bag.**

## The rule (from Runner Guide §Rules)
> Runners will be listed as **DNFing** until the aid station cutoff time **OR** the sweepers have passed the aid station where the runner dropped — **WHICHEVER HAPPENS FIRST**. This means runners may re-enter the race after choosing to DNF.

## What this means for you
You can:
1. Drop at an AS (turn in SPOT, fill out drop form)
2. Sleep, eat, recover
3. Change your mind
4. Return to the SAME AS, pick up your SPOT
5. Continue racing

**This is legal. This is the rules. This is a tool.**

## When to use this lever
- Sleep monsters at 3 AM, can't think straight, want to quit
- Heat exhaustion that you think will resolve with 2 hrs of rest
- GI distress that you think will pass with food + rest
- Mental fatigue that will recover with sleep

## When NOT to use it
- Section I (hard pull) symptoms — drop for real
- Beyond the AS cutoff (can't re-enter past cutoff)
- After sweepers have passed (can't re-enter)
- When the timeline math says you'll miss the next cutoff anyway

## The decision
At the AS where you want to drop, ask yourself:
1. **Can I sleep here for 1–3 hrs and still make the next cutoff?** Check the page in `02-aid-station-atlas/`.
2. **What do I need to recover?** Food, sleep, ice, anti-emetic?
3. **Am I in a hard-pull (Section I) state?** If yes → real drop.

If sleep + food + recovery + good math → **DNFing, not DNF.**

## Pre-committed: this option exists
Knowing this lever exists changes the decision math. You're not deciding "finish or quit" — you're deciding "rest now or rest later."

## Logistics of DNFing → re-entering
1. Tell AS captain: "I'm dropping but may return"
2. Turn in SPOT
3. Fill out drop form
4. Sleep / eat / recover INSIDE the AS area (don't wander off)
5. When ready: tell AS captain, retrieve SPOT, retrieve bib, re-enter course
6. Continue

## What you lose by DNFing-then-returning
- Time. Real time. Watch the cutoffs.
- Possibly placement (irrelevant for you).
- Some pride for the moment — irrelevant in 6 months.

## What you gain
- A real chance at finishing what would've been a non-finish.
- The story of having used the lever.

**This is on the back of every card so you read it once before every drop bag is opened.**
