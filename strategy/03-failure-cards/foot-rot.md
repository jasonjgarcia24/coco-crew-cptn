# 🦶 FOOT CARE

**Print + laminate. Copy in every drop bag.**

## The rule
**Foot care is NOT optional. It is scheduled.** At 250 mi, neglected feet end races. Treat foot care like fueling — a non-negotiable AS task.

## Mandatory foot care AS (at minimum)
- Lane Mtn (M32.5) — first opportunity post-Cottonwood→Crown King section
- Camp Wamatochick (M67.4) — after first long sleep 🚿
- Mingus Mtn (M107.2) — after climb, post-shower
- Sedona Posse (M159.1) — pre-climb to Schnebly
- Fort Tuthill (M211.0) — fresh shoes from CK drop bag
- Walnut Canyon (M227.1) — last full reset

## Foot care ritual (~10 min)
1. Sit down. Shoes off.
2. Socks off — inspect for hot spots, blisters, maceration
3. Wipe feet dry — towel, paper towel
4. Foot powder (Gold Bond / similar) on feet AND inside dry socks
5. Lube hot spots / between toes (Squirrel's Nut Butter / Body Glide)
6. Tape any blister or developing blister (Leukotape preferred over moleskin)
7. Drain ONLY blisters >1 cm with sterile pin, leave skin intact
8. Fresh dry socks on
9. Shoes back on — different pair if rotation strategy
10. Lace check — not too tight (swelling), not too loose

## Hot spot treatment (caught early)
- Stop walking
- Lube + tape + cover
- Resume — but reduce pace if heat-affected

## Established blister
- Drain with sterile pin if >1 cm and tense
- Apply Leukotape OVER drained blister, don't pop fully
- Foam padding around if pressure point
- Track — re-check at every AS

## Macerated feet (white, swollen, soft)
- Dry feet hard at AS — towel + powder + 5+ min air dry
- Fresh socks
- Reduce moisture: change socks more frequently, dust with powder, consider hydrophobic socks

## Toenail bruising / loss
- Don't try to remove a partially-attached nail mid-race
- Tape over to stabilize
- Treat post-race

## When to STOP and address vs. push
- Hot spot caught in 30 sec: STOP NOW, treat in 3 min, save your race

- Bone-deep pain that walking changes: medic AS, real assessment
