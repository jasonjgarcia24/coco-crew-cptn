# Heat Strategy

**Cocodona is May in central Arizona. Heat IS the race in the first 36.6 miles.**
**Anchor everything else around the Cottonwood Creek → Crown King section.**

## The Cottonwood→Crown King section (M7.4 → M36.6)
- 25 mi between Cottonwood Creek (M7.4) and Crown King (M36.6)
- Hottest, hardest, most exposed on course
- Mid-pack 10 hrs. Back-of-pack 13+ hrs.
- Two intermediate water stations: M10.4 + M24.6, **1L cap each**
- Lower elevations: 2,000–4,000 ft → daytime temps potentially 90s+ °F

## Heat ramps ~8:00–8:30 AM Mon
Race starts 5:00 AM. **First ~3 hours (5:00 → 8:00 AM) are dawn-cool.** This is when you can push:
- **Start → Cottonwood (M0 → M7.4): target 9:30–10 min/mi**, banking time before heat ramps
- **Cottonwood → section start (M10.4 water station)**: starts arriving ~6:30 AM (FAST), still cool-ish
- **Heat starts biting around M15+ as sun rises and exposure ramps**
- Strategic intent: minimize time above 80°F by pushing pre-heat then settling into climb pace

The full 50k-by-heat strategy: get past Lane Mtn (M32.5) before peak afternoon heat. Climbs after Cottonwood will naturally be slower; that's expected. The acceleration is in the first 7.4 mi only.

**See `03-failure-cards/heat-cottonwood-to-crown-king.md` for the action card.**

## Pre-race heat prep (week-of)
- Check forecast Sun 03-May-2026 for Mon high temp at low elevation
- Pre-cool: cold breakfast, ice in bottles, no caffeine pre-start
- Sun gear: light/white hat, sunglasses, white sleeves, neck shade, sunscreen reapplied hourly

| Section | Heat exposure | Strategy |
|---------|--------------|----------|

| Sedona → Schnebly climb (M159–176) | FAST = Tue PM (warm), A-GOAL = Wed early AM (cool) | Slow steady climb; salt + fluid pre-loaded at Sedona Posse |

## Rules for ALL heat sections
1. **Drink BEFORE thirsty.** Thirst = already dehydrated.
2. **Salt every 30 min** regardless of how you feel. **700+ mg sodium/hr in extreme heat** (slight bias up from 500 floor).
3. **Walk every uphill.** Pace below threshold. **Heart rate ceiling: 165 bpm.**
4. **Shade > everything.** Sit in shade if available. Lose 5 min, save the day.
5. **Pour water on hat/sleeves** for evaporative cooling — but only if drinking water isn't at risk.
6. **Pee color: pale yellow target.** Clear = drinking too much (hyponatremia). Dark = too little.

## Heat ceiling (per contract Section IV)
- **Heart rate ceiling: 165 bpm**
- HR should mostly remain MODERATE for this distance — 165 is the ceiling, not the target
- IF HR sustained >165 → walk, ice, drink, restart only after pulse drops below 150

## Sodium math (cross-ref `05-fueling-baseline.md`)
**Sweat rate baseline:**
- Day: 1.0 L/hr × 757 mg/L sodium = **757 mg/hr lost**
- Night: 0.5 L/hr × 757 mg/L sodium = **378 mg/hr lost**

**Target intake:**
- Day moderate: 500 mg/hr (slight deficit -257 mg/hr — acceptable for short stretches)
- **Day extreme heat (Cottonwood→CK, Verde Valley): 700–900 mg/hr** (track to balance)
- Night/cold: 500 mg/hr (slight surplus — fine)

**Sources to hit 700–900 mg/hr in extreme heat:**
- 1× Personal mix (500 mg) + 1× Liquid IV (510 mg) per hour = 1,010 mg/hr — sufficient
- OR: 1× Personal mix (500 mg) + 1× salt cap (~250 mg, depending on brand) + 1× Neversecond (200 mg) per hour = ~950 mg/hr

## Red flags — STOP and assess
See `03-failure-cards/heat-cottonwood-to-crown-king.md`:
- Stop sweating in heat → heat exhaustion / heat stroke risk → SHADE NOW, ICE NOW
- Mental fog, slurred speech → SHADE, ICE, fluids w/ salt
- Cramping that doesn't resolve in 5 min → salt + slow walk
- No urination in 4+ hrs → drink to color
- Goosebumps in heat → heat stroke imminent → STOP

## Cool-section transition
Once past Crown King (M36.6) AND nighttime, switch to cool-weather rules:
- Cold Weather Gear required (see gear-check card)
- Reduced fluid intake: 0.5 L/hr night (vs 1.0–1.25 L/hr day)
- Sodium intake holds at 500 mg/hr (sweat rate drops at night)
- Hot fluids at AS (broth, coffee)
- Layer management — start cold, warm up moving

## What to avoid
- Caffeine before / during the Cottonwood→Crown King section (vasodilation / dehydration risk)
- Plain water without salt during heat
- New foods on race day
- Big meals at AS during heat — small frequent ingestion
- Sitting in the sun at AS — find shade always
- Black/dark-colored running gear during exposed sections
